Factorial of a number

Problem Statement Program Animation Video Test Cases Practice

The factorial of a non-negative integer is a mathematical operation that calculates the product of all positive integers less than or equal to that number. It is denoted by the exclamation mark (!) following the number.
For example, the factorial of 5 is written as 5!, and it is calculated as:

5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120.

In general, the factorial of a number n, denoted as n!, is the product of all positive integers from 1 to n. The factorial function can be defined recursively as: 

                n! = n × (n-1)!

with the base case being 0! = 1.

It's important to note that factorials grow very rapidly as the input number increases. The factorial of even moderately large numbers quickly becomes extremely large, which can lead to numerical overflow in computer calculations. In such cases, specialized algorithms or approximation techniques are used to handle factorials.
